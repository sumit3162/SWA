Smart Ward Assistant

Transforming rural healthcare with affordable Edge-AI monitoring.

Overview

The Smart Ward Assistant is an innovative healthcare technology project designed to improve patient safety and emergency response in rural hospitals and Primary Health Centers (PHCs). The system was created with one clear mission: to make smart healthcare affordable, accessible, and reliable for villages and low-resource medical environments.

This project combines IoT, Edge-AI, Embedded Systems, and Wireless Communication technologies to continuously monitor patients in real time without depending on expensive ICU infrastructure or stable internet connectivity.

The Inspiration Behind the Project

The idea for the Smart Ward Assistant was born from real experiences in rural Maharashtra. I observed how rural hospitals struggle daily with limited staff, lack of equipment, and delayed emergency response.

In many rural hospitals, a single nurse manages an entire ward of patients. During emergencies, the nurse may be handling injections, medicines, labor room cases, or other critical tasks simultaneously. Because there are no continuous monitoring systems, patients are checked manually after long intervals.

One incident deeply inspired this project. A patient admitted with breathing complications was resting in a ward late at night. Slowly, the patient’s oxygen level started dropping. However, there was no smart monitoring device to alert the staff. At the same time, the nurse was occupied handling another emergency case.

The patient could not call for help. There was no alarm. No monitoring system. No real-time alert. By the time the patient’s condition was noticed, the situation had already become serious.

That moment revealed the harsh reality of rural healthcare: the issue was not lack of care from nurses or doctors. The issue was the absence of affordable monitoring technology.

Problem Statement

  • High cost of ICU monitoring systems
  • Limited availability of doctors and nurses
  • Poor internet connectivity
  • Frequent power interruptions
  • Delayed emergency detection
  • Lack of continuous patient monitoring

Traditional ICU monitors are expensive and often cost between ₹1 lakh and ₹3 lakh per unit, making them impossible for small hospitals to deploy at scale. As a result, many patients remain unmonitored between manual checkups, increasing the risk of unnoticed emergencies.

Our Solution

The Smart Ward Assistant is a low-cost, internet-independent patient monitoring system that continuously tracks vital health parameters such as heart rate and oxygen saturation (SpO₂). The system uses wearable sensor nodes connected wirelessly to an intelligent Edge-AI processing hub.

Unlike traditional cloud-based systems, the Smart Ward Assistant works completely offline using ESP-NOW wireless communication technology. This makes it ideal for villages and hospitals with unstable internet connectivity.

Example alert: “Emergency detected on Bed Number 4.”

Technologies Used

  • ESP32-WROOM-32
  • ESP32-S3-BOX-3
  • MAX30102 / MAXREFDES117 Sensor
  • Li-Po Battery
  • TP4056 Charging Module
  • ESP-NOW
  • FreeRTOS
  • MQTT
  • Edge-AI Algorithms
  • Web Dashboard
  • Voice Synthesis System

System Architecture

  1. Wearable sensor captures patient vitals
  2. ESP32 processes physiological signals
  3. Data transmitted wirelessly via ESP-NOW
  4. Edge-AI hub analyzes risk conditions
  5. Voice alert generated during emergency
  6. Dashboard displays patient information for doctors and nurses

This architecture ensures low latency, high reliability, and complete offline functionality.
