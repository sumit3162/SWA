Research Overview

Smart Ward Assistant – Edge-AI Based Rural Healthcare Monitoring System

An offline, low-cost research platform for continuous patient monitoring in rural hospitals and PHCs.

Research Domain

  • Internet of Things (IoT)
  • Embedded Systems
  • Edge Artificial Intelligence (Edge-AI)
  • Rural Healthcare Technology
  • Smart Patient Monitoring

Research Problem

Rural hospitals and Primary Health Centers (PHCs) often lack affordable ICU monitoring systems. Due to limited medical staff, high equipment costs, poor internet connectivity, and lack of continuous patient monitoring, many emergencies are detected late, increasing the risk of complications and patient fatalities.

Traditional ICU monitoring systems are expensive and require stable infrastructure, making them difficult to deploy in rural healthcare environments.

Research Objective

  • A low-cost patient monitoring system
  • An internet-independent healthcare solution
  • A real-time emergency alert system
  • A smart monitoring platform for rural hospitals

The system aims to improve patient safety while reducing dependency on expensive ICU infrastructure.

Proposed Research Solution

The Smart Ward Assistant uses wearable IoT sensor nodes, ESP32-based Edge-AI processing, offline wireless communication, and voice-based emergency alerts to continuously monitor patient vitals such as heart rate and oxygen saturation (SpO₂).

The system processes data locally using Edge-AI instead of cloud computing, ensuring low latency and reliable operation in rural areas.

Research Innovation

  • 100% Offline Healthcare Monitoring
  • Edge-AI Risk Assessment
  • Localized Voice Emergency Alerts
  • Low-Cost IoT Infrastructure
  • Multi-Patient Monitoring System
  • Wireless Wearable Architecture

Unlike cloud-based systems, the Smart Ward Assistant operates independently without requiring continuous internet access.

Technologies Used

Hardware

  • ESP32-WROOM-32
  • ESP32-S3-BOX-3
  • MAX30102 / MAXREFDES117 Sensor
  • Li-Po Battery System

Software

  • FreeRTOS
  • ESP-NOW Protocol
  • MQTT
  • Edge-AI Algorithms
  • Web Dashboard

Methodology

  1. Physiological signal acquisition
  2. Signal filtering and preprocessing
  3. Heart rate and SpO₂ extraction
  4. Edge-AI risk analysis
  5. Wireless data transmission
  6. Emergency voice alert generation
  7. Dashboard visualization

The system uses Photoplethysmography (PPG) signals for vital extraction and local AI-based risk scoring.

Experimental Results

  • 96% Monitoring Accuracy
  • Less than 1.5 Second Alert Latency
  • 98% Network Reliability
  • 24+ Hour Battery Backup

Testing confirmed stable operation even in low-resource rural environments.

Research Significance

  • Affordable healthcare technology can achieve medical-grade performance
  • Rural hospitals can benefit from smart monitoring systems without expensive infrastructure
  • Edge-AI can reduce emergency response delays significantly

The project helps bridge the healthcare technology gap between urban and rural regions.

Applications

  • Rural Hospitals
  • PHCs
  • Emergency Wards
  • Elderly Monitoring
  • Home Healthcare
  • Temporary Medical Camps
  • Disaster Relief Healthcare

Future Research Scope

  • ECG Monitoring
  • Blood Pressure Monitoring
  • TinyML Predictive Analytics
  • AI-Based Early Warning Systems
  • Solar-Powered Wearable Devices
  • Smart Healthcare Cloud Integration

Conclusion

The Smart Ward Assistant proves that smart healthcare systems can be affordable, scalable, and reliable for rural healthcare environments. By combining IoT, Edge-AI, and offline wireless communication, this research provides a practical solution for improving patient safety and healthcare efficiency in underserved regions.
