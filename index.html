About rural hospitals

Rural hospitals and Primary Health Centers (PHCs) provide essential care in villages and remote areas but often face shortages of medical staff, limited equipment, unreliable internet and power, and high patient loads. These constraints increase the risk of delayed treatment and preventable complications.

  • Shortage of doctors and nurses
  • Lack of ICU monitoring systems
  • Poor internet and power infrastructure
  • Delayed emergency response
  • High equipment costs
  • Limited access to advanced technology

Smart Ward Assistant

The Smart Ward Assistant is a low-cost, offline patient monitoring system designed for rural hospitals and PHCs. It continuously monitors heart rate and oxygen levels using wearable IoT sensors and performs Edge-AI risk analysis locally.

Key features

  • Real-time patient monitoring
  • Offline operation using ESP-NOW
  • Voice alerts for emergency detection
  • Low-cost deployment (~₹10,000)
  • Edge-AI based risk analysis
  • Wireless wearable sensor nodes
  • Doctor dashboard and mobile alerts (optional)

Benefits

By providing continuous monitoring without depending on internet connectivity, the system reduces response times, improves patient safety, and supports medical staff with localized alerts and concise risk information.

  • Affordable technology
  • Internet-free operation
  • Faster emergency response
  • Continuous 24/7 monitoring

Technologies

  • ESP32-WROOM-32, ESP32-S3-BOX-3
  • MAX30102 / MAXREFDES117 sensors
  • ESP-NOW wireless protocol
  • Edge AI processing, TinyML
  • MQTT & web dashboard (optional), FreeRTOS

Applications & future scope

Ideal for PHCs, emergency monitoring, post-operative care, elderly monitoring, home healthcare, mobile health camps, and disaster response. Future versions may add ECG, temperature, blood pressure, solar-powered wearables, and advanced predictive TinyML models.
